Client
Re Company

Re Company is a sustainable consumer brand offering reusable, plastic-free food storage. I joined a cross-functional team of 11 to shape how the brand communicated its environmental mission and practical benefits, then helped lead the rapid pivot from B2B to B2C when COVID-19 collapsed the original go-to-market.

The Brief

The pandemic not only disrupted Re Company's go-to-market strategy; it invalidated it overnight. A brand built around restaurant partnerships suddenly had no restaurant partners. The pivot to B2C was necessary, but consumer resistance around hygiene, convenience, and price created more hurdles in a category that was already crowded. We had to earn trust from an audience that had grown skeptical of greenwashing, inconvenience dressed as virtue, and of reusable products at a time when single-use felt safer.

Strategy & Positioning

The first decision was what not to lead with. Environmental guilt had become the default for sustainability brands, creating distance rather than desire. Re Company needed to position sustainability as:

Aspiration, not obligation.

The repositioning centred on three ideas: aspirational and accessible care for the environment, hygiene, and modernity. Hygiene, in particular, wasn't just a real product strength; it was a direct response to the anxiety COVID had introduced into the reusables category.

Creative Direction

My initial direction leaned into urban textures, contrasting the cleanliness of the packaging against the grime around it. As the cultural moment came into focus, I realized that approach would feel out of step. Consumers were anxious about hygiene and skeptical of guilt-led sustainability messaging.

I moved the direction toward an optimistic, modern future: modular packaging that solved the problems consumers had accepted as "just the way things were." Staining, retained smell, looking like your grandmother's tupperware, melting in the dishwasher, harmful plastics tolerated because the product did the job.

The Result

The repositioning supported a successful B2C pivot that translated directly into commercial performance: over 150,000 product sales worldwide and market expansion into New Zealand, Australia, and Southeast Asia.

This proved that the brand was coherent enough to travel: consistent in its values, flexible enough in its expression to resonate across cultures.
